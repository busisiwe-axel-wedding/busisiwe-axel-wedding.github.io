Our recommendations

Now that you've seen a glimpse of where we live and celebrated with us, you might be thinking about extending your stay in South Africa. If you have a few extra days to spare, both Pretoria and Cape Town offer incredible experiences that go far beyond the wedding weekend. To help you plan, we've put together a few of our personal favourite activities, hidden gems and great restaurants - consider this our 'insider guide' for those who want to truly immerse themselves in the food, history, nature, and culture of our beautiful country.

Pretoria

Where to eat

  • Capital Craft - known for serving quality craft beers and delicious pub food in a relaxed setting.
  • SMOKE - boasts immaculate aesthetics and ambience, serving great food and drinks.
  • The Blue Crane Restaurant - Recommended for those who want a glimpse of nature and South African architecture while dining around a lapa.
  • The View Rooftop Bar - a spectacular choice for watching the orange hues of the hills and treelines of Pretoria.

    • Neighbourhoods

  • Hazelwood (old east precicnt) - vibrant, trendy dining hub centred around "The Village," known for modern restaurants, bars, and a buzzing, social atmosphere.
  • Menlyn Main - Pretoria's modern commercial and retail centre, featuring the enormous Menlyn Park Shopping Centre, major corporate buildings, and high-rise hotels.
  • Waterkloof - Jacaranda tree spotting central, an affluent, stately suburb known for its large, elegant homes, quiet tree-lined streets, and upmarket guesthouses with city views.

    • Nature and exploration

  • Hartebeespoort - join a boat cruise or visit the aerial cable way to see the hartebeespoort dam from above, or explore the surrounding nature reserves.
  • Dinokeng Game Reserve - Gauteng's only Big 5 nature reserve close to Pretoria.

    • Landmarks and history

  • The Union Buildings
  • Voortrekker Monument
  • Freedom Park

    • Cape Town

    We have a larger list of recommendation in our current home, Cape Town. Here are some of our favourites in no particular order:

    Rooftop and sunset spots

  • Radisson Red Rooftop Bar - 360 Views of the CBD of Cape Town.
  • Chinchilla Rooftop Cafe and Bar - tropical vibes, in a chic rooftop oasis, right next to Camps Bay beach.
  • Tiger's Milk Camps Bay - right by the beachside with affordable prices and the best sunset.
  • Baptiste Rooftop bar - exclusive rooftop bar serving 360 views away from the hustle of the city

    • African Cuisine

  • Seven Colours Eatery - known for serving authentic, flavourful South African home-style coooking focusing on the "seven colours" plate (a Sunday meal tradition).
  • Marco's African Place - a vibrant restaurant offering a wide range of African dishes (from game to West African options) paired with live music and entertainment. Try to name all the flags!
  • Meeting Point - a popular spot offering hearty, affordable Tanzanian cuisine, evoking nostalgic tastes of Southern Africa. This is very much your local joint, hidden in the middle of the CBD.
  • KwaMai Mutsa - cosy unpretentious place known for Southern African braai (barbecue) and meaty dishes, served in a lively, authentic atmosphere.

    • Fine Dining

  • La Colombe - a world-renowned experience offering multi-course, theatrical menus with French and Asian influences in a stunning setting. (Book far in advance!)
  • Marble Restaurant - located in the V&A Waterfront, it offers an excellent high-end dining experience with the art of cooking with fire.
  • ëlgr - contemporary cuisine with a strong emphasis on Scandinavian and local seafood preparations

    • Beers and vibes

  • The House of Machines - a legendary bar/café/clothing store combo known for excellent coffee by day and coctails and beers by night. A classic Cape Town hangout, they offer live music every night, and on Sundays there's live music playing at 1PM on the lovely side street where they are located. A must!
  • Aegir Project - relaxed and unpretentious beer house with live music and great burgers.
  • Woodstock Brewery - great local beers in the cool urban area of Woodstock.
  • Shackleton Brewery - tiny brewery in Paarden Eiland worth a visit for their quirky range of beers.

    • Art galleries

  • Youngblood - a lively, dynamic gallery space that hosts exhibitions, performances, and has a serene ground-floor café and venue.
  • Zeitz Mocaa - a cultural institution, at old silos at the V&A Waterfront. Spectacular setting and art.
  • The Iziko South African National Gallery - hosts a ton of great South African masterpieces.

    Neighbourhoods

  • Bo-Kaap (where we live) - famous for its brightly coloured houses and cobblestone streets, this historic area is rich in culture and history.
  • De Waterkant - a trendy, more pricier cosmopolitan area adjacent to the CBD and Green Point, featuring chic boutiques, stylish apartments, and design studios. This place also has the best beef restaurant in town, Don Armando.
  • Gardens - the true heart of the city, home to the famous Company's Garden, the National Gallery, and many museums. It is densely packed with trendy cafés, bars, and bistros, offering a perfect mix of history, greenery, and urban cool.
  • Sea Point - an Atlantic Seaboard suburb famous for the Sea Point Promenade, a long, scenic walkway perfect for jogging and sunset strolls, and lots of restaurants and bars.
  • Woodstock and Observatory - two adjacent historical, industrial suburbs that have transformed into a hub for ecletic street art, design, and food. Home to The Old Biscuit Mill and many galleries and street art.

    • Nature and exploration

  • Table Mountain National Park - ascend via the rotating cable car or hike the iconic Platteklip Gorge, or take the route from Kloof Nek via Kasteelsport. The views of the city, mountains, and ocean are a must-do.
  • Cape Peninsula Drive - a full-day drive. Take the breathtaking Chapman's Peak Drive (toll) to the Cape of Good Hope and Cape Point. This will be part of the roadtrip on Wednesday.
  • Kirstenbosch Botanical Gardens - widely regarded as one of the most beautiful botanical gardens in the world, set against the slopes of Table Mountain. The park offers another route to the top of Table Mountain, through Skeleton Gorge.

    • Wine Routes

  • Hemel and Aarde Valley - (Creation Wines, Ataraxia, Barto Eksteen) - located near Hermanus, this route is celebrated for its cool-climate Pinot Noir and Chardonnay. The estates here offer high-end, contemporary tasting experiences.
  • Constantia Route (Beau Constantia, Klein Constantia, Groot Constantia, Constantia Uitsig) - the oldest wine route in South Africa, located just 20 minutes from the CBD. Known for its historic estates and excellent white wines, especially Sauvignon Blanc.
  • Franschhoek, Paarl and Stellenbosch - check out the wine tram! These regions form the historical core of the Winelands, known for their warmer climates which produce robust, full-bodied wines (especially Cabernet Sauvignon and Shiraz). They are essential visits for their stunning Cape Dutch architecture and deep French Huguenot heritage.
  • Riebeek Kasteel - a smaller, less crowded route known for its Mediterranean feel, olive farms, and exceptional Shiraz.

    • Landmarks and history

  • District Six Museum - a powerful and moving museum dedicated to the history of the forced removals during the apartheid era, told through the memories of former residents.
  • Robben Island - a moving, essential visit. Take the ferry from the V&A Waterfront to the island where Nelson Mandela was imprisoned. Tours are often led by former political prisoners.
  • Iziko Museums - a collective of museums covering history, art, and natural history. Includes the Slave Lodge, the South African Museum and Bo-Kaap museum.
  • The Company Gardens - originally established in the 1650s to supply fresh produce to ships, it's now home to squirrels, the National Parliament and cafes.

    • For the adventurous

    One of the amazing properties of Cape Town, is that there are spectacular places only a few hours away in a car. This list is of our absolute highlights.
  • Creation Wines - voted the best wine farm in Africa. Has beautiful art, in addition to a fantastic lunch menu.
  • Fram Wines - a remote vineyard on top of a mountain, named after the Norwegian explorer Fridtjof Nansens's ship "Fram". Only way to visit is via booking. Then you'll be seated with a lot of strangers, sharing food and wine in a biblical setting.
  • Fryer's Cove - a wine farm located on the rugged West Coast, with wines that taste like fresh sea water. Absolutely stunning. One of the few places you can eat abalone in South Africa legally.
  • Wolfgat - the best restaurant we've ever been to in South Africa. A tiny establishment, that only serves seafood and local wines are served. Located in beautiful Paternoster. Absolutely amazing. Book well in advance.
  • Tankwa Padstal - drive an hour from Cape Town, and you are in the lush winelands. Drive for another hour, and you are in the middle of the Tankwa desert. This place is a fantastic watering hole, that serves beers, pub grub and amazing stories. If you are truly adventurous, continue along the R355 (the longest gravel road in South Africa) all the way to Calvinia. This is a theurapeutic drive, with endless desert scenery. The road is a bit rough, but should be doable in any rental car, as long as you don't go too fast.
  • Cederberg Wines - a wine farm in the majestic Cederberg mountains. They do not have to use any pesticides because of their remote location. Stay at the local Cederberg Oasis for an incredibly chilled stay.
